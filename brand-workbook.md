# Locally Sauced — Brand Development Workbook

---

## PHASE 1: BRAND STRATEGY

### 1. The "Why"

**Brand Purpose:**
To celebrate and support independent restaurants — the places that give our neighbourhoods their character and bring communities together through food.

**Brand Vision:**
A Locally Sauced edition on kitchen shelves in every city — making independent restaurants a celebrated part of every community.

**Brand Mission:**
We partner directly with independent restaurants, collect their real signature recipes, and present them in beautifully designed, city-specific binders — giving restaurants exposure and giving home cooks a connection to the places they love.

---

### 2. Target Audience

**Primary — "The Food Lover" (self-purchase):**
- Age: 30–50
- Location: UK-wide, expanding county by county
- Income: Mid-to-high
- Settled in their area, knows and loves their local restaurants
- Couples or young families who cook regularly at home

**Secondary — "The Gift Buyer":**
- Age: 20–50 (kids buying for parents, or buying for friends/partners)
- Looking for something unique and personal
- Drawn to the local/city-specific angle as a thoughtful touch

**Psychographics:**
- Care about supporting local and independent businesses
- Enjoy cooking at home but want to go beyond the usual recipes
- Eat out regularly and have favourite local spots
- Value quality over quantity — would rather have one nice thing than five cheap ones
- See food as social — cooking for friends, family dinners, trying new things together
- Take pride in where they live and their local area

**Pain Points:**
- Independent restaurants can't compete with the marketing budgets and reach of chains — they rely on footfall and word of mouth
- Home cooks love their local restaurants but can't recreate those dishes — the recipes aren't available anywhere
- Generic cookbooks have no personal connection to the places people actually eat
- Finding a unique, meaningful gift for a food lover is hard — most options feel impersonal

---

### 3. Brand Positioning & USP

**Competitors:**
1. Big-name chef cookbooks (Ottolenghi, Jamie Oliver, Nigella) — the default cookbook purchase
2. MOB Kitchen / recipe content brands — modern alternative to traditional cookbooks
3. Experience/foodie gifts (restaurant vouchers, food boxes) — competing for the same gift occasion

**Unique Selling Proposition:**
We're the only cookbook featuring real, uncompromised recipes from the independent restaurants on your doorstep — giving home cooks something personal to where they live, and giving restaurants a marketing channel they've never had.

---

### 4. Core Brand Values

1. **Celebrate Local** — Everything we do starts with supporting independent restaurants and the communities around them.
2. **Authenticity** — Real recipes, real chefs, real stories. We never water down what makes a dish or a restaurant special.
3. **Quality** — From the recipe cards to the binder, everything is designed to be kept, displayed, and handed down.
4. **Community** — Building connections between restaurants and the people who live around them.
5. **Accessibility** — Bringing restaurant-quality cooking into any home kitchen.
6. **Heritage** — Family recipes passed down through generations deserve to be preserved and shared — not lost when a restaurant closes its doors.

---

## PHASE 2: BRAND IDENTITY

### 5. Brand Personality & Voice

**Brand Personality:**
The mum on the street who everyone knows and trusts. She's an incredible cook, she feeds every kid in the neighbourhood, and her door is always open. She knows every family, every story, and she's the reason the street actually feels like a community. She doesn't just care — she gets things done.

**Traits:** Nurturing, Capable, Welcoming, Community-proud, Generous

**Brand Voice:**
Warm and conversational, like talking to someone who genuinely cares. Knowledgeable but never preachy. We speak with pride about our restaurants and our communities — always encouraging, never lecturing.

**We sound like:** A trusted neighbour recommending their favourite local spot — warm, genuine, and confident in what they're sharing.

**We NEVER sound like:** A corporate chain, a pretentious food critic, a hard-sell marketing campaign, or virtuous and self-righteous about supporting local.

---

### 6. Visual Identity

**Color Palette:**
Warm, rich, and premium — like a restaurant interior with candlelight. Burgundy and gold give it a classic, timeless feel. Cream keeps it inviting rather than heavy.

- Primary: Burgundy (#5C1A1A)
- Secondary: Gold (#C9A84C)
- Background: Cream

**Typography:**
Classic serif for headings and the brand name — gives it a cookbook/editorial feel. Clean sans-serif for body text — modern and easy to read. The combination says "premium but approachable."

**Imagery:**
Warm, atmospheric food photography — close-up shots of dishes with natural light, chefs at work in their kitchens, restaurant interiors with character. The binder itself styled as a kitchen shelf object. Candid and real, never overly polished stock photography.

---

## PHASE 3: BRAND EXECUTION

### 7. The Brand Story & Elevator Pitch

**Elevator Pitch:**
We help home cooks bring their favourite local restaurants into their kitchen by partnering directly with independent chefs to collect their real signature recipes — presented in beautifully designed, city-specific binders.

**Brand Story:**
Locally Sauced started with a simple frustration: the best food in any city is hiding in plain sight. While chains spread across every high street with big budgets and wide reach, the independent restaurants — the ones run by families, the ones with recipes passed down through generations — rely on word of mouth and hope for the best. We thought they deserved more. So we started knocking on doors, sitting down with chefs and owners, and collecting the dishes they're most proud of. Not simplified versions — the real recipes. We turn them into beautifully printed cards, collected in a premium binder, one edition per area. It's a cookbook your community built — and every copy sold puts an independent restaurant in front of the people who live around it.
